[Type 2 Diabetes Complications in Ethnic Minority Compared with European Host Populations](https://doi.org/10.1136/bmjdrc-2024-004345)

**[Main Findings]**

- Ethnic minority populations with type 2 diabetes showed higher risks of several diabetes-related complications than European host populations.
- Differences were particularly evident for renal and cardiovascular outcomes.
- The findings highlight persistent ethnic inequalities in diabetes complications and the need for targeted prevention and clinical management.

[Interaction between Lifestyle Factors and Polygenic Risk in Hypertension](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.5379908)

**[Main Findings]**

- Higher polygenic risk was associated with increased hypertension risk.
- **Stress exposure was itself associated with hypertension risk, identifying stress as a relevant lifestyle-related exposure alongside traditional behavioural risk factors.**
- BMI showed the strongest interaction with polygenic risk among the lifestyle factors examined; overweight and obesity amplified genetic susceptibility on the additive scale.
- The findings support studying genetic susceptibility together with behavioural and psychosocial exposures when evaluating hypertension risk.

Anna D. Argoty-Pantoja, Zekai Chen, **Felix Reichelt**, Tian Xie, Chris H. L. Thio, Harold Snieder. 2025. *Interaction between Lifestyle Factors and Polygenic Risk in Hypertension: Insights from the Lifelines Cohort Study.* **SSRN preprint**. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.5379908) · [SSRN](https://ssrn.com/abstract=5379908)
