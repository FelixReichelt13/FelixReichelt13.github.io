I am a PhD candidate in **Genetic Epidemiology** at the **University Medical Center Groningen (UMCG) / University of Groningen** and a registered **Epidemiologist B**. My doctoral research is embedded in the [**Stress-in-Action consortium**](https://stress-in-action.nl/), a seven-university collaboration investigating how genetic vulnerability and stress exposures shape health across the life course.

My research focuses on why individuals differ in their exposure to stressful experiences and how genetic liability and stress jointly relate to cardiometabolic health. Specifically, I aim to:

1. Quantify the **heritability and genetic architecture of stress exposures and vulnerability traits**.
2. Characterize **genetic and environmental covariance between stress exposures and personality traits** using twin- and family-based designs.
3. Investigate **gene × stress-exposure and gene × stress-response interplay in cardiometabolic outcomes**.
4. Integrate **polygenic risk scores, clinical predictors, and large-scale cohort data** for etiological research and risk modelling.

My experience includes genetic and epidemiological analyses in large-scale population and twin-family datasets, including [**Lifelines**](https://www.lifelines-biobank.com/) and the [**Dutch Twin Register**](https://tweelingenregister.vu.nl/nl). I apply twin and family modelling, polygenic risk scores, GWAS-derived data, Mendelian randomization, and longitudinal epidemiological methods. My broader background spans molecular cell biology, biomedical data science, proteomics, and clinical genetic diagnostics, giving me experience across both wet-lab and computational research environments.
