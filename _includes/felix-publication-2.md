[Interaction between Lifestyle Factors and Polygenic Risk in Hypertension](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.5379908)

**[Main findings]**

- BMI showed the strongest interaction with polygenic risk: overweight and obesity amplified genetic susceptibility to hypertension on the additive scale.
- Stress exposure was associated with higher odds of hypertension, highlighting psychosocial stress as a relevant lifestyle-related risk factor.
- The findings support evaluating genetic susceptibility together with behavioural and psychosocial exposures.
