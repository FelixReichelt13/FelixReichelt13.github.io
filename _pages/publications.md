<div class='paper-box-text' markdown="1">

[Type 2 Diabetes Complications in Ethnic Minority Compared with European Host Populations](https://doi.org/10.1136/bmjdrc-2024-004345)

[**Main findings**]

- Ethnic minority populations with type 2 diabetes showed higher risks of several diabetes-related complications than European host populations.
- Risk differences varied across ethnic groups and complication types.
- The findings highlight persistent inequalities in diabetes outcomes and the need for targeted prevention and clinical management.

<div class='paper-box-text' markdown="1">

[Interaction between Lifestyle Factors and Polygenic Risk in Hypertension](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.5379908)

[**Main findings**]

- BMI showed the strongest interaction with polygenic risk: overweight and obesity amplified genetic susceptibility to hypertension on the additive scale.
- Stress exposure was associated with higher odds of hypertension, highlighting psychosocial stress as a relevant lifestyle-related risk factor.
- The findings support evaluating genetic susceptibility together with behavioural and psychosocial exposures.

</div>
