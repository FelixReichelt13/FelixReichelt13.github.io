### Selected figure

<img src="/images/Publication_2.png" alt="Relative excess risk from the interaction between polygenic risk score and BMI on hypertension" style="max-width: 700px; width: 100%; height: auto;">

*Relative excess risk from the interaction between polygenic risk score and BMI on hypertension.* The figure summarises the individual associations of polygenic risk and BMI categories with hypertension, alongside additive interaction quantified using the relative excess risk due to interaction (RERI). Overweight and obesity were associated with higher hypertension risk, particularly in individuals with higher polygenic risk, and BMI showed the most pronounced interaction with genetic susceptibility among the lifestyle factors investigated. These findings suggest that adiposity may amplify genetic susceptibility to hypertension, although the interaction effects contributed only modestly to explained variance.

- Anna D. Argoty-Pantoja, Zekai Chen, **Felix Reichelt**, Tian Xie, Chris H. L. Thio, Harold Snieder. 2025. [Interaction between Lifestyle Factors and Polygenic Risk in Hypertension: Insights from the Lifelines Cohort Study](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.5379908). *SSRN preprint*.
